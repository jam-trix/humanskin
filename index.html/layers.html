About us
 

At first glance, skin might seem like a simple outer shell — just a protective cover for our bodies. But peel back the surface, and you'll discover a complex, multi-layered system working constantly to keep you safe, cool, hydrated, and even alive. The human skin isn't just the largest organ; it's a high-tech armor made up of layers that each play a unique role. From the tough, water-resistant outer shield to the deep, living tissues filled with nerves and blood vessels, your skin is a biological masterpiece. Let’s dive beneath the surface and explore the fascinating layers of your skin — where science meets survival.

 

Skin Layer

 

Epidermis:

The outermost layer, composed of tightly packed epithelial cells. Contains the primary protective structure, the stratum corneum. It is further divided into five layers: stratum corneum, stratum lucidum, stratum granulosum, stratum spinosum, and stratum basale. The stratum corneum is the outermost layer and acts as a barrier to water loss and environmental exposure.

 

Dermis:

The middle layer, a fibrous layer that supports and strengthens the epidermis. Contains blood vessels, hair follicles, sweat glands, and other structures. The dermis is further divided into two layers: the papillary layer and the reticular layer.

 

Hypodermis (Subcutaneous Tissue):

The deepest layer, composed mainly of loose connective and fatty tissues. Provides insulation and stores energy. Supplies nutrients to the skin.
